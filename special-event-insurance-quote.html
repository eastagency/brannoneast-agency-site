Special Event Insurance in Georgia

You have spent months planning your event — the venue, the vendors, the guest list. Special event insurance protects that investment and shields you from liability if something goes wrong on the day. Whether you are planning a wedding in North Georgia, a corporate event in Cartersville, or a private party at a rented venue, event liability coverage is fast to obtain, affordable, and increasingly required by Georgia venues.

What Special Event Insurance Covers

Event liability coverage protects you if a guest is injured or if property is damaged during your event — and the venue or injured party pursues a claim against you as the host. Host liquor liability covers incidents related to alcohol service at private events where alcohol is served but not sold. Event cancellation and postponement coverage reimburses non-refundable deposits and pre-paid expenses if you are forced to cancel or reschedule due to illness, severe weather, vendor failure, or other covered causes.

Who Needs Special Event Insurance?

Wedding couples (most Georgia venues now require proof of liability coverage from the renting party), corporate event planners, non-profit organizations hosting fundraisers or galas, and anyone hosting a private party at a rented facility. Even events in private backyards may warrant coverage if you are serving alcohol or expecting a significant number of guests.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does special event insurance cover?

Event liability coverage protects you if a guest is injured or property is damaged during your event. Cancellation coverage reimburses non-refundable deposits if you must cancel due to illness, severe weather, or a vendor going out of business.

Does my Georgia venue require event liability insurance?

Most Georgia wedding and event venues now require a certificate of liability insurance naming them as an additional insured. Brannon can issue one quickly, often within 24 hours of binding coverage.

How far in advance should I buy event insurance?

As early as you book the venue. Cancellation coverage only protects deposits and costs incurred after the policy is purchased. The sooner you buy it, the more of your investment is protected.
