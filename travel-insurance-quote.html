Travel Insurance for Your Next Trip

Whether you're headed on a family vacation, a cruise, or an international trip, a lot can go wrong between booking and boarding — a cancelled flight, a sudden illness, a lost suitcase, or a trip cut short by an emergency back home. Travel insurance protects the money you've already put down and gives you a safety net once you're on the road.

What Travel Insurance Covers

A typical plan includes trip cancellation and interruption coverage that reimburses prepaid, non-refundable trip costs; emergency medical and dental coverage while you're away from home; emergency medical evacuation and repatriation; baggage delay and loss coverage; and trip delay benefits for meals and lodging when you're stuck waiting on a delayed flight or connection.

What Your Other Coverage Misses

Most health insurance plans provide little to no coverage once you leave the country, and none of them reimburse you for a cancelled trip. Credit card travel benefits are often limited and full of exclusions. A standalone travel policy is built specifically to cover the financial risks of travel that your other policies simply don't touch.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does travel insurance cover?

Most travel insurance plans cover trip cancellation and interruption, emergency medical care and evacuation, delayed or lost baggage, and trip delays. Comprehensive plans bundle all of these; cheaper plans may cover only one or two categories.

How much does travel insurance cost?

Travel insurance typically costs 4% to 10% of your total trip cost. A $4,000 trip might cost $160 to $400 to insure, depending on your age, trip length, and how much cancellation and medical coverage you choose.

Can I get travel insurance for a cruise?

Yes. Cruise-specific plans often add benefits standard travel policies miss, like missed port coverage, cabin confinement benefits, and emergency medical evacuation from a ship at sea. Brannon can point you to a plan built for cruising.

When should I buy travel insurance?

Buy within 10 to 21 days of making your first trip payment to unlock time-sensitive benefits like pre-existing condition waivers and "cancel for any reason" upgrades. You can still buy later, but you may lose access to those extras.
