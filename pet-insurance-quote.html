Frequently Asked Questions

Does pet insurance cover pre-existing conditions?

Most pet insurance policies do not cover pre-existing conditions, meaning illnesses or injuries that existed or showed symptoms before coverage started. Getting coverage while your pet is young and healthy locks in the best rates and broadest protection.

Is pet insurance worth it in Georgia?

For most pet owners, yes. A single emergency vet visit in Georgia can cost $1,500 to $5,000 or more. A monthly premium of $25 to $60 can make those bills manageable and prevent you from making difficult financial decisions about your pet's care.

What does pet insurance cover?

Prudent Pet covers accidents, illnesses, hereditary and congenital conditions, chronic conditions, prescription medications, diagnostic testing, surgery, and hospitalization. Optional add-ons include wellness care for vaccines, annual exams, and routine checkups.

How much does pet insurance cost per month in Georgia?

Most dog owners pay $30 to $80 per month and cat owners pay $15 to $40 per month, depending on breed, age, deductible, and reimbursement level. Premiums rise as pets age, so enrolling early locks in lower rates.
